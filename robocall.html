Project 4 : FCC Robocalls Complaints

Introduction

The Federal Communication Commission (FCC) is an independent government agency of the Unites Stated who regulates interstate and international communications by radio, television, wire, satellite and cable. They are also responsible for enforcing communication law. Individual's complaints on spam callers are filed with the Consumer Help Center since October 2014 until 2018. Everyday, the average spam callers are rising constantly. With individual's constant busy schedules, spam callers can be considered disturbing at some moment. The aim of this project is to analyze the behavior of spam callers such as who, when, how and frequency of their calls.

Software used: SQL Developer, MySQL, Tableau, MS Excel

Dataset Explanation & Aim of Analysis

Fig 4.1 shows the variables in this dataset. This dataset contains 15 variables and 1.5 million attributes. From all these variable, this project will take a closer look at the date complaint was made, location of complaint, date/time of the issue, form of issue and Caller ID. Some of the challenges present on this dataset are: (1) 45% of the record's attribute contains null, leaving only 55% of the data that can be analyzed. (2) Some entries also shows "none" which should be taken care off like the null attributes. (3) In addition, data such as day of the week should be relevel initially before querying. This analysis will focus on:

Analysis 2: Frequency of Calls Againt Time

The second analysis of these spam callers are based on time. The three charts below shows the frequency based on a yearly, hourly, and day of the week manner respectively. As for the yearly chart, we can conclude that frequency increases on average. The hourly chart tells us that spam callers peaked at 10am and 11am. At 12pm, frequency dropped as usually it is lunch time. Then it increase again after lunch at 1 pm and gradually decrease towards the end of working hours. Lastly from the third chart shows that Wednesday and Thursday to be the most busiest day fron unwanted calls. Based of this data, we can conclude that number of spam callers are rising there are higher probabilities that you get called on Wednesday and Thursday in comparison to the rest of the week, between 10am to 1pm.

Analysis 3: Nationwide Frequency

Fig 4.9 shows the heat map of spam callers across United States. As we can see, the frequency of calls happened the most at California, New York, Texas, Florida, and Washington. Therefore, we will take a closer look at these states on the called ID and origin.

Conclusion

To sum up this analyses, the main objective of this project is to figure out when to ignore spam caller phones and know their potential phone number. By understanding them, users can be more familiar with spam callers and can simply prepare counter measures to reject calls. This analyses can also be useful for telemarketers so they know when to call their recipients.

To enhance the result of this analyses, some of the possible data that can be used together with this data are having the days of the week in the dataset so that day of the week can be queried through MySQL. Having more data on people that actually benefit from telemarketing and telemarketing data from other countries are good sources of identifying spams. In addition, knowing the effectiveness of robocall and telemarketing could really benefit telemarketers.

Overall, the analyses in this dataset tells us that spam callers mostly target their recipients on Wednesday and Thurday during 10am-1pm. Washington is one of the top 5 state with spam callers, where the city of Seattle are often targeted by cals and telemarketers from Washington itself and California with Caller IDs 206, 425, 253, 360, 502 and 202.
